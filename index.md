## Rachel A. Neville 

Hello! I'm a postdoctoral researcher in the [math department](http://math.arizona.edu/) at the [University of Arizona](https://www.arizona.edu/), where I am studying **Applied Topology and Pattern Formation**. 

My main area of research is in topological data analysis, specifically persistent homology.  (The AMS ran a nice feature column on persistent homology that you can read [here.](http://www.ams.org/publicoutreach/feature-column/fc-2016-12?utm_content=bufferc2bd1&utm_medium=social&utm_source=twitter.com&utm_campaign=buffer))  I use topological and geometric techniques to study dynamical systems and pde's. This gives a unique lens through which to investigate and characterize patterns of real and simulated data.  I'm currently working with [Alan C. Newell](http://math.arizona.edu/~anewell/) studying pattern forming systems.
